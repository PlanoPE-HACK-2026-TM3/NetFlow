What if I only put 15% down instead of 20%?
With 15% down on $360k, your loan is $306k at 6.94% → monthly P&I = $2,034. Add taxes ($380) + insurance ($120) = PITI $2,534. At est. rent $3,180, your monthly cash flow drops to +$284 vs +$612 at 20% down. Cap rate unchanged at 6.8%. Risk stays MEDIUM.
How does that compare to 10% down?
10% down → P&I = $2,160, PITI = $2,660. Cash flow turns negative at −$68/mo. PMI adds ~$175/mo making it −$243. I'd stick with 15–20% for this property given the MEDIUM risk flag.
